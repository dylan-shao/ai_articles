## Copilot usage metrics now include more of your active users
- Source: GitHub Changelog
- Author: N/A
- Publish date: 2026-06-15
- URL: https://github.blog/changelog/2026-06-15-copilot-usage-metrics-now-include-more-of-your-active-users/

GitHub updated Copilot enterprise usage reports and API to incorporate server-side confirmed activity in addition to client telemetry. Active paid users previously missed due to network, proxy, or client reporting gaps will now appear in single-day and 28-day active user reports.

Why it matters: This is less about product capability and more about fixing the measurement substrate for organizational adoption. Platform and engineering leaders rely on active-user metrics for rollout tracking, renewals, seat reclamation, team benchmarking, and ROI analysis; if the underlying telemetry undercounts usage, governance decisions are distorted. The update improves DAU coverage, but it also highlights that server-side augmentation does not carry the same rich IDE/feature/model/LOC detail as client telemetry, so top-line adoption and granular behavior analysis should be treated separately.

Key takeaways:
- Enterprise Copilot active-user reporting should better reflect real usage, especially in environments affected by proxies, networking, or client settings.
- Server-side signals improve coverage but do not fully replace the granular analytical fields available from client telemetry.
